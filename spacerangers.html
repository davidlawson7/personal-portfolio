Space Rangers

An interactive storytelling game to assist in early learning.

SPACE RANGERS


Space Rangers is an interactive storytelling game centred around the idea that player driven stories can assist in early learning. The story itself changes based on the users choices and actions throughout the game. To play you use physical gestures like pulling, pushing and hitting to complete tasks or challenges. You also talk to it and use your words to answer questions and tell it what you want to do.


The setting is this. You are a Space Ranger and have crash landed on a mysterious planet. You have nothing but your wits about you. To get off the planet you have to explore it, talk to locals and overcome challenges in the hope that you'll somehow fix you ship.





Kinect Technology


Kinect is used to detect and track the player's movements and physical gestures.



Voice Commands


Players make in game choices using their speech - they tell it what they want to do.



Branching Paths


Branching story paths are a defining aspect of Space Rangers. Player's choices and actions determine what path the story takes.

What and Why?


It was mentioned above that this games original intent was to use interactive storytelling as a means to assist with learning in the early years. This is intended to be done by creating an immersive experience that the user is actually a part of, rather than just being a spectator. Doing this is intended to engage children for longer, thus providing amply opportunity to impart some message onto them.


The message in a wider context could be anything the creator(s) would want to teach children. For this project we aimed to create and implement puzzles which exercised their general reasoning and problem solving skills. It also has the opportunity to teach that there are consequences for your actions as not all paths through the game lead to the same thing.
